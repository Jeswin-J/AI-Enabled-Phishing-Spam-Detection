    Good to Know

    • What is spam email?

      Spam refers to unwanted or unsolicited messages, typically sent in bulk, often for advertising purposes. These messages can include emails, text messages, or comments on websites.

    • What is phishing?

      In simple terms, phishing is a type of cyberattack where attackers trick people into giving away their sensitive information, such as passwords, credit card numbers, or personal details. This is usually done by sending deceptive emails or messages that appear to be from trusted sources, like banks or companies. The goal of phishing is to steal valuable information or gain unauthorized access to accounts.

    • How can I recognize spam emails?

      Spam emails often contain generic greetings, poor grammar or spelling, requests for personal information, urgent calls to action, or suspicious attachments or links. They may also come from unfamiliar or suspicious email addresses.

    • What should I do if I receive a suspicious email?

      If you receive a suspicious email, do not click on any links, download attachments, or provide personal information. Instead, mark the email as spam or phishing (if your email provider offers this option) and delete it. You can also report it to your organization's IT department or the relevant authorities.

    • How can I protect myself from phishing attacks?

      To protect yourself from phishing attacks, be cautious of unsolicited emails, verify the legitimacy of the sender before clicking on any links or providing information, use strong, unique passwords for online accounts, and enable multi-factor authentication when available.

    • What are some common phishing techniques?

      Common phishing techniques include email spoofing (where the sender's address is forged to appear as a trusted source), deceptive links (URLs that appear legitimate but lead to malicious websites), and social engineering (manipulating individuals into divulging sensitive information through psychological manipulation).

    • What should I do if I accidentally clicked on a phishing link or provided information?

      If you accidentally clicked on a phishing link or provided sensitive information, take immediate action to protect yourself. Change your passwords for any compromised accounts, notify your bank or credit card company if financial information was involved, and consider running a virus scan on your device to check for malware.
